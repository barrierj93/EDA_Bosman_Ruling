# Bosman Ruling: FIFA World Champions by Continent

An interactive data visualization exploring how the 1995 Bosman Ruling reshaped the global balance of power in football — measured through FIFA Club World Cup and FIFA World Cup titles won by South American and European sides from 1930 to 2024.

---
![alt text](bosman_ruling.png)

## What it shows

The visualization tracks the **cumulative number of world titles** (Club + National Team) won per continent across nine decades of football history. Three key moments:

- **1930** — First FIFA World Cup
- **1960** — First Intercontinental Cup
- **1995** — The Bosman Ruling

Before 1995, South American clubs and national teams held a 7-title lead — a 28.57% symmetric advantage. In the three decades since, European sides have accumulated 29 titles compared to just 7 for South America.
